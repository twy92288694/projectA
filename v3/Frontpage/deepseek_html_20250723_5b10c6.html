History of Maldives

The Maldives has a rich and diverse history dating back over 2,500 years. The islands were first settled by seafarers from India and Sri Lanka around 500 BCE, bringing with them Buddhism which would remain the dominant religion for over 1,000 years.

In 1153 CE, the Maldives converted to Islam under the influence of Arab traders, and the country has remained a Muslim nation ever since. The Portuguese briefly occupied the islands from 1558 to 1573, but were driven out by local hero Muhammad Thakurufaanu Al Auzam.

The Maldives became a British protectorate in 1887 but maintained internal self-governance. The islands gained full independence in 1965, and in 1968 the sultanate was replaced by a republic. Today, the Maldives is known worldwide for its stunning natural beauty and thriving tourism industry.

Maldivian Culture

Language & People

The Maldivian people are ethnically diverse, with influences from Arab, African, and South Asian cultures. The official language is Dhivehi, an Indo-Aryan language with its own script called Thaana. English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

Cuisine

Maldivian cuisine is centered around fish, especially tuna, and coconut. Traditional dishes include mas huni (shredded smoked fish with coconut), garudhiya (fish soup), and roshi (flatbread). Due to Islamic traditions, alcohol is only available in resort islands.

Traditions & Arts

Traditional Maldivian arts include bodu beru (big drum) music and dance, lacquer work, and mat weaving. The culture is deeply influenced by Islam, with religious festivals like Ramadan and Eid being major celebrations. The traditional dress for women is the libaas.

Top Diving Sites

Banana Reef

Intermediate

One of the first dive sites discovered in the Maldives, famous for its overhangs, caves, and stunning coral formations. Home to groupers, barracudas, and moray eels.

Manta Point

Beginner

As the name suggests, this site is famous for its manta ray cleaning stations. Between May and November, you can see dozens of these gentle giants gliding through the water.

Fish Head

Advanced

An exciting dive site with strong currents that attract large pelagics like gray reef sharks, hammerheads, and tuna. The coral formations are spectacular with many overhangs.

Weather & Climate

Climate Overview

The Maldives enjoys a tropical monsoon climate with warm temperatures year-round (average 28-32°C). There are two distinct seasons:

Dry Season (November-April)

Characterized by low rainfall, low humidity, and calm seas. The northeast monsoon (Iruvai) brings sunny days perfect for beach activities and diving.

Wet Season (May-October)

The southwest monsoon (Hulhangu) brings more rain and higher humidity, but showers are usually brief. This is the best time for surfing with bigger waves.
